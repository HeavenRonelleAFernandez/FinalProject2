In the Philippines


According to a recent study, most regions in the Philippines have fortunately recorded significant decrease in poverty incidence in 2023, with 11 out of 18 regions having so. Though, many regions still remain to live in poverty, causing it to remain as a serious problem in the Philippines.

According to income data, 3.0 million households, or 10.9 percent of Filipino families, were categorized as poor in the preliminary 2023 FIES results. The percentage of households with earnings below what is required to meet basic necessities for food and non-food items is referred to as the poverty incidence. Individually, 17.54 million Filipinos, or 15.5% of the population, were living in poverty in 2023.

Causes


An obvious reason is the country's rapid increase in population throughout the years, reaching 106 million this year. This of course means more mouths and feed and more resources to use up for the nation to thrive.

Throughout the past 40 years, the Philippines' low to moderate economic development has limited job creation and put many families in low-paying, precarious jobs. It is now more difficult to eradicate poverty and enhance living standards because of the government's reduced investment in infrastructure, healthcare, and education as a result of this slow growth.
